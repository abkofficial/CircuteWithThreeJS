Principle

The elementary AC generator works on the principle of Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction.

When a conductor cuts magnetic flux, an emf is induced in it.

Construction (As shown in Simulation)

  1. Magnetic Field
    Strong uniform magnetic field produced by North (N) and South (S) poles.
  2. Rectangular Coil (Armature)
    Placed between magnetic poles.
    Consists of N turns.
    Rotates with angular velocity ω.
  3. Slip Rings (R¹, R²)
    Connected to the ends of the rotating coil.
    Allow AC to be taken out without breaking the circuit.
  4. Carbon Brushes (B¹, B²)
    Collect current from slip rings.
    Connected to external load / galvanometer.
  5. Galvanometer / Load
    Indicates magnitude and direction of induced emf.

Working of Elementary AC Generator

  • When the coil rotates in a magnetic field:
    • Its sides cut magnetic flux
    • Induced emf is produced according to Faraday's Law.

Expression for Induced EMF

Magnetic flux linked with the coil:
Φ = B A cos θ

Induced emf:
e = -N dΦ / dt
e = N B A ω sin θ

Maximum emf:
Emax = N B A ω

Hence,
e = Emax sin(ωt)
